Fight Meta — Instructor Terms, Standards &
Content Agreement

1. Platform Overview
   Fight Meta is a structured combat sports learning platform providing step-by-step instruction,
   sparring breakdowns, and guided progression for boxing, Brazilian jiu-jitsu, wrestling, and mixed
   martial arts. Fight Meta enables instructors to distribute instructional content globally while building
   recurring revenue and expanding their personal brand.
2. Instructor Eligibility & Payment Setup
   To publish content on Fight Meta, instructors must be approved through invitation or application,
   provide valid bank account details for payouts, provide identification or tax information if requested,
   and maintain professional instructional standards. Instructors are responsible for complying with all
   tax obligations related to their earnings.
3. Revenue Model & Payments
   Revenue Split
   Instructors receive 90% of revenue generated from their content. Fight Meta retains 10% to cover
   platform development, hosting, streaming infrastructure, payment processing, and platform growth.
   Pricing Control
   Instructors set their own course pricing. Fight Meta may provide pricing guidance and
   recommendations.
   Payment Schedule
   Instructor earnings are calculated and paid every 7 days via bank transfer to the instructor’s
   nominated account. Fight Meta reserves the right to adjust payout schedules with reasonable
   notice.
   Minimum Payout Threshold
   Earnings must reach SGD 75 before payout is processed. Balances below this threshold roll over to
   the next payout period.
   Payment Holds
   Fight Meta may temporarily withhold payouts if disputes, fraud, suspicious activity, or policy
   violations occur. Disputes, chargebacks, or processing fees may be deducted from future instructor
   earnings.
4. Refund Policy
   All purchases on Fight Meta are considered final. Refunds are not offered except where required by
   applicable law or payment provider regulations. Students retain lifetime access to purchased
   content even if an instructor later leaves the platform.
5. Content Ownership & Licensing
   Ownership
   Instructors retain full ownership of their content.
   Platform License
   By uploading content, instructors grant Fight Meta a worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license
   to host, stream, transcode, optimise, market, promote, and display content to paying students. This
   license remains active for students who purchased access even if the instructor removes content or
   leaves the platform.
6. Content Exclusivity
   Instructional content uploaded to Fight Meta may not be re-uploaded, resold, distributed publicly, or
   published on competing instructional platforms while the same content is hosted on Fight Meta.
   Instructors may continue teaching or producing unrelated instructional material outside the platform.
7. Course Structure Requirements
   Courses must follow Fight Meta’s structured learning format. Volumes represent major skill
   categories and must include descriptive titles. Volumes are divided into Sub-Volumes (lessons),
   and each lesson must be uploaded as an individual video. Lessons should focus on one technique,
   concept, or related sequence and progress logically from fundamentals to advanced application.
   Estimated duration must be provided for all Volumes and lessons.
8. Content Production Standards
   All instructional videos must meet the following requirements: • Minimum 1080p resolution • Clear
   audio (lapel or lavalier microphone recommended) • Stable camera positioning • Adequate lighting
   with clear technical visibility • Clean and professional training environment Fight Meta reserves the
   right to request re-recording if standards are not met.
9. Content Submission Process
   Instructors currently submit instructional material through Google Drive using the following
   structure: Instructional (Main Folder) → Volume (Sub Folder) → Individual Lesson Videos
   Instructors must clearly label folders, upload final edited files, include required metadata, and
   maintain backup copies of source content. Fight Meta is not responsible for preserving or returning
   original source files.
10. Content Removal & Platform Rights
    Fight Meta may remove or restrict content that violates intellectual property rights, breaches
    platform policies, fails quality standards, creates legal or reputational risk, or is associated with
    instructor misconduct.
11. Student Access Rights
    Students who purchase courses receive lifetime access to purchased content. If an instructor
    leaves Fight Meta, existing students retain access and Fight Meta retains rights to host purchased
    content for those students.
12. Analytics & Performance Data
    Fight Meta provides instructors with performance analytics such as sales performance,
    engagement metrics, completion tracking, and viewing analytics. Aggregated data may be used to
    improve platform features and student experience.
13. Platform Development & Policy Updates
    Fight Meta may update platform features, payment systems, and content standards over time.
    Instructors will be notified of material policy changes affecting earnings, licensing, or content
    requirements.
14. Instructor Conduct
    Instructors are expected to maintain professional behaviour, provide accurate instruction, avoid
    misleading marketing claims, respect intellectual property rights, and represent the combat sports
    community responsibly.
15. Termination
    Either party may terminate participation on the platform. Upon termination, new content sales may
    be disabled, students retain lifetime access to purchased courses, and outstanding earnings remain
    subject to payout thresholds and dispute review.
16. Independent Contractor Status
    Instructors operate as independent contractors and are not employees, partners, or agents of Fight
    Meta. Instructors are solely responsible for income tax reporting, insurance, business registration
    requirements, and compliance with local regulations.
